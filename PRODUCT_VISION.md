# WorldGraph Product Vision

## The Core Problem

**Current State**: WorldGraph is a powerful graph visualization tool, but it's missing the "why" and "what next" that makes products sticky.

**The Gap**: Users can explore data, but they don't:
- Discover unexpected insights
- Know what to do with what they find
- Have a reason to come back
- Understand the value immediately

## Product Vision: "The Insight Discovery Engine"

WorldGraph should be the tool that helps people **discover connections they didn't know existed** and **turn those connections into actionable insights**.

---

## Core Value Propositions

### 1. For Researchers
**"See the hidden connections in your field"**
- Import papers from PubMed/arXiv
- Auto-discover citation networks
- Find unexpected research connections
- Identify knowledge gaps

### 2. For Knowledge Workers
**"Connect your ideas and find patterns"**
- Import from Notion, Obsidian, Roam
- Auto-link related concepts
- Discover themes in your notes
- Build personal knowledge graphs

### 3. For Analysts
**"Map relationships and find insights"**
- Import company data
- Visualize business relationships
- Identify influence networks
- Discover market patterns

### 4. For Educators
**"Show how ideas connect"**
- Create interactive learning maps
- Show historical connections
- Visualize cause and effect
- Build narrative timelines

## User Journey

### First-Time User
1. **Landing**: See value proposition clearly
2. **Onboarding**: Choose a template or import data
3. **First Insight**: Within 30 seconds, see something interesting
4. **Exploration**: Guided to explore more
5. **Action**: Export or share what they found
6. **Return**: Get notified about new insights

### Power User
1. **Import**: Bulk import from multiple sources
2. **Analyze**: Use analytics to find patterns
3. **Discover**: Use AI to find hidden connections
4. **Share**: Create stories and presentations
5. **Collaborate**: Work with team on shared graphs
6. **Automate**: Set up integrations and workflows

## Competitive Differentiation

### vs. Obsidian/Roam
- **Better visualization** (they're text-based)
- **More structured** (they're free-form)
- **Better for sharing** (they're personal)

### vs. Neo4j Browser
- **More user-friendly** (they're developer-focused)
- **Better insights** (they're just visualization)
- **More accessible** (they require technical knowledge)

### vs. Kumu/Miro
- **More intelligent** (they're manual)
- **Better for knowledge** (they're general purpose)
- **More automated** (they require manual work)
